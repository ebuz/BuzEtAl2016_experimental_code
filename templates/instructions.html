This experiment consists of three phases, followed by a short questionnaire.

In phase one, you will listen to a short speech from an amateur debater. In this speech, the speaker will present his/her stance on a current political issue. Listen carefully to the speech, and form an impression of the speaker and their argument. You will be asked to describe them later.

In phase two, you will be presented with ten pictures, one at a time. When each picture is displayed it will automatically start recording audio. Please describe what is happening in each picture using one sentence. Then press "Finish" to advance to the next picture. Also, please avoid pronouns (words like he/she, him/her, etc). For example, if the picture was of a boy smiling at a girl, you could say "The boy is smiling at the girl" but not "He is smiling at her."

Phase three is a brief survey. This survey consists of questions about the speech that you heard in phase one and questions about you as a participant. The data we collect with the survey will be non-identifying. It is very important to us that you try to answer the questions in the survey to the best of your abilities. Rushing through the survey will make your data completely useless for our research purpose.

All together the experiment will take about 20-25 minutes. Thank you for your participation

Please do not take breaks in the middle of the experiment. It is important that you do the experiment in one go.

By accepting this HIT, you confirm that you have read and understood the consent form, that you are willing to participate in this experiment, and that you agree that the data you provide by participating can be used in scientific publications (no identifying information will be used). Sometimes it is necessary to share the data elicited from you — including sound files — with other researchers for scientific purposes (for replication purposes). That is the only reason for which we will share data and we will only share data with other researchers and only if it is for non-commercial use. Identifying information will never be shared (your MTurk ID will be replaced with an arbitrary alphanumeric code).
