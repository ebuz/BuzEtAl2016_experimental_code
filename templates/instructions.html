Picture Description

This experiment consists of three parts, followed by a short questionnaire.

In the first part, you will listen to a short speech from an amateur debater. In this speech, the speaker will present his/her stance on a current political issue. Listen carefully to the speech, and form an impression of the speaker and their argument. You will be asked to describe them later.

In the second part, you will be presented with ten pictures, one at a time. When each picture is displayed it will automatically start recording audio. Please describe what is happening in each picture using one sentence. Then press "Finish" to advance to the next picture. Also, please avoid pronouns (words like he/she, him/her, etc). For example, if the picture was of a boy smiling at a girl, you could say "The boy is smiling at the girl" but not "He is smiling at her."

Part three is a brief survey. This survey consists of questions about the speech that you heard in part one and questions about you as a participant. The data we collect with the survey will be non-identifying. We will not share your information with any third parties (the human subject review board at the University of Rochester monitors that we follow these rules).

All together the experiment will take about 20-25 minutes.

Please do not take breaks in the middle of the experiment. It is important that you do the experiment in one go.

By accepting this HIT, you confirm that you have read and understood the consent form, that you are willing to participate in this experiment, and that you agree that the data you provide by participating can be used in scientific publications (no identifying information will be used).

Once you press Start, these instructions will disappear, so make sure you understand them fully before you start.
