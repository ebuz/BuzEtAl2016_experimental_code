  • Eligibility

    Language requirements

    You must be a native speaker of American English. If you have not spent almost all of your time until the age of 10 in the United States, you cannot participate. If you have a non-American accent we will not be able to pay you. For example, British or Indian English is not permissible for this experiment.

    Computer requirements

    This experiment requires that your browser support Flash and javascript.

    This experiment requires that your computer have a working microphone. We recommend you use a head mounted microphone with the microphone a few inches from your mouth or a desktop microphone that you keep a consistent distance from your mouth. Please try not to whisper or yell. {% if not preview %} If this is the first time you're visiting this page, you might see a dialogue asking you to give us permission to record from your microphone while you are participating in this experiment.   • Experiment length

    In total, this experiment will take about 45 minutes. This experiment is split up into multiple parts. This first part includes instructions and tests to make sure you're able to complete the rest, this should take about 5 minutes. After this we will have you do 6 practice trials which should take about 2 minutes. Then you will do the real experiment, made up of 90 trials, which should take about 25 minutes. Finally, we will ask you to fill out a survey which should take about 10 minutes.

    Please wait for your partner to be ready to start the next trial.

    • First we'll find and connect you to a partner.

    • Once connected, the trials will start. You'll see a little cross on the screen.

    • Next you'll see three words. You should read all of them. Below a timer bar will appear too.

    • After a moment, one will be highlighted. The timer bar will also start.

    • Read the highlighted word, in this case 'guide', to your partner. They will hear your with very little delay but remember you won't hear them.

    • During or after you finish talking your partner will respond.

    • If the timer bar finishes then the trial will end even without a response from your partner. Although your partner is instructed to wait for you to speak they may respond early. Don't let this frustrate you.

    • If your partner picks the right word you'll see the word you said highlighted in green.

    • If your partner picks the wrong word you'll see the word they picked in red. If they pick the wrong word don't get frustrated, just continue to the next trial.

    • After the trial ends you'll have 30 seconds to start the next one. Press the space bar to start the next trial. If you wait too long the experiment will end and the survey will start. There are 90 trials total.

    • Your partner will hear you but it's a one way connection; you can't hear them.

    • Your partner will see the same thing.

    • Your partner will see the same words but not necessarily in the same order.

    • Your partner's timer bar will start too but nothing else will change on their screen.

    • Your partner will listen to you. Their goal is to click on the word you are saying.

    • Your partner will click what they are hearing as quickly as possible.

    • They are trying to respond as quickly and accurately as possible so they may not wait for you to finish.

    • Your partner will not be told if they got it right or not.

    • Your partner will be waiting for you so try not to make them wait too long.
